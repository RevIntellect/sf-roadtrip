A routed two-day walk through the city, grouped so you never double back, then the northbound leg in the rental van — up the Marin coast and on into the redwoods.

Before you go — field notes

  • 01
    Time the bridge. Save the Golden Gate for a clear window — fog usually burns off by late morning. Parking is scarce and break-ins common, so rideshare in.
  • 02
    Dress in layers. Summer is the cool, foggy season here — wind off the Pacific keeps the western neighborhoods chilly even in July. Locals call the fog Karl. Bring a jacket.
  • 03
    Getting around. Skip driving downtown — parking is scarce and pricey. Tap a phone or a Clipper card on Muni and BART; cable cars are a flat $8, exact cash or the MuniMobile app.
  • 04
    Skip the Lombard queue. Walk the pedestrian stairs beside the crooked block; the car line is slow and you see less from inside.
  • 05
    Swan Oyster Depot. Lunch-only with a 2:30 close, cash only, and shut Sundays (optional, Day 2). Make it a priority or skip it.
  • 06
    The drive north. The far-north redwoods are ~170 miles up the coast, so plan a night on the way. Short on time? Muir Woods, just over the Golden Gate, has the same giant redwoods a fifteenth of the distance away.

By bike

The Mission

San Francisco's oldest neighborhood, grown up around Mission Dolores (1776), the oldest standing building in the city. For generations it has been the heart of the Latino and Chicano community, and that history is painted right on the walls — the murals of Balmy Alley and Clarion Alley, blocks of taquerias, and the Mission-style burrito that carried the name worldwide.

Leg 03 · The Van

Road Trip North

Out of the city and up the coast, then the long push into the redwoods.

Short on time? Stay close

You don't have to drive 170 miles to stand under giant redwoods. Muir Woods is barely a fifteenth of the distance — just over the Golden Gate into Marin — and every bit as awe-inspiring, no $7.75-a-gallon fill-up required. And for raw coastline, Jenner — a small town up Highway 1 north of Muir Woods, where the Russian River meets the sea — is every bit as dramatic as Big Sur.

Day 4 · Redwood Country

2

Confusion Hill

MiddayLeggett · US-101

A 1949 roadside gravity house deep in redwood country, about 3.5 hours further north up 101. A tilted Gravity House, a mountain train ride, and the world's tallest freestanding redwood chainsaw carving. Open 10 to 5 daily; the train pauses in winter.

Open in Maps Official site
3

Avenue of the Giants

AfternoonHumboldt · US-101

A 31-mile scenic parkway through Humboldt Redwoods State Park, running alongside US-101 — the old-growth highlight of the whole trip, and free. Pull off at Founders Grove or the Rockefeller Forest to walk among the tallest of the coast redwoods. Easy to fold into the drive right around Confusion Hill.

The drive in between

The Marin coast and the redwoods are about 170 miles apart, so make it two days, not one slog.

Fast way: cut inland to US-101 and run it north, roughly 3.5 hours.

Scenic way: stay on Highway 1 up through Point Reyes, Bodega Bay, and Mendocino. Highway 1 dead-ends into 101 right near Leggett, basically at Confusion Hill's doorstep. In a van with time to spend, this plus a night in Mendocino or Fort Bragg is the better trip.

Either way, detour onto the Avenue of the Giants just before Confusion Hill — the old-growth redwood parkway paralleling 101 is the highlight of that stretch, and it's free.
